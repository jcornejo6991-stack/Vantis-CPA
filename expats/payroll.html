Common Situations

We've seen this before

Employment situations abroad create confusion around SE tax obligations. These are the most common scenarios we resolve.

Situation 1
Expat working remotely for a US employer, unsure about SE tax
If you're a W-2 employee of a US company working remotely from abroad, you're generally not subject to SE tax — but your FICA treatment depends on your employment agreement and whether a Totalization Agreement applies to your host country. The details matter.
Situation 2
American abroad with both US and foreign employment income
When you have a W-2 from a US employer and wages from a foreign employer in the same year, the FICA and SE tax treatment of each income stream is separate. We analyze both and prevent overpayment of Social Security contributions.
Situation 3
Expat consultant with multiple 1099s and foreign contract wages
Multiple US clients issuing 1099s, combined with foreign contract income, creates a complex SE tax calculation. We consolidate all self-employment income, apply the correct deductions, and document the FEIE interaction with SE tax correctly.
