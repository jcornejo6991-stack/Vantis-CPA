Common Situations

We've seen this before

Expats with US business interests face a specific set of complications. These are the scenarios we handle regularly.

Situation 1
Expat freelancer who needs a US LLC for client contracts
Many US clients won't engage foreign contractors directly. A US LLC gives you a domestic entity for invoicing and banking — but it needs to be properly maintained and reported on your US return. We handle both sides.
Situation 2
American abroad with a US rental property held in an LLC
A single-member LLC holding US real estate is simple enough on its own — but when the owner is abroad, state filing requirements, rental income reporting, and FBAR considerations for the LLC's accounts all need attention.
Situation 3
Expat who owns a foreign corporation triggering Form 5471
If you own 10% or more of a foreign corporation, you may be a US shareholder in a Controlled Foreign Corporation. Form 5471 is one of the most complex disclosure forms in the tax code — and the penalties for non-filing are steep.
