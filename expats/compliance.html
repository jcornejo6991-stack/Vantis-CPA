What's Included

The full range of expat disclosure services

From annual FBAR filing to multi-year catch-up procedures, we handle the disclosures that create the most anxiety for expats — with a clear process and no judgment about how you got here.

🏦
FBAR (FinCEN 114) Filing
Annual Report of Foreign Bank and Financial Accounts for US persons with aggregate foreign account balances exceeding $10,000 at any point during the year. We file the FinCEN 114 directly through BSA E-Filing, with maximum balance tracking across all accounts.
📋
FATCA Form 8938 Filing
Statement of Specified Foreign Financial Assets, required when your foreign financial assets exceed the applicable threshold. Filed with your tax return — we ensure thresholds are correctly applied and all specified assets are reported.
🔄
Streamlined Filing Procedures
For expats with delinquent or incorrectly filed returns, the Streamlined Foreign Offshore Procedures allow you to come into full compliance — filing three years of returns and six years of FBARs — with significantly reduced (or zero) penalties if non-willful.
🪪
ITIN Applications for Foreign Spouses
If you're married to a non-US citizen who doesn't have a Social Security Number, an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number is required for joint filing. We prepare the Form W-7 application and coordinate the required documentation.

Common Situations

We've seen this before

Compliance backlogs and disclosure situations are far more common than people realize. You're not alone in this, and there is a path forward.

Situation 1
Expat with 5+ years of unfiled FBARs
This is the most common compliance situation we handle. The Streamlined Foreign Offshore Procedures were specifically designed for expats in this position — non-willful non-filers can come into compliance with zero FBAR penalties. We've guided dozens of clients through this process without incident.
Situation 2
American married to a non-US citizen needing an ITIN for joint filing
Filing jointly can significantly reduce your tax liability — but it requires your foreign spouse to have either a Social Security Number or an ITIN. We prepare the W-7 application, identify the required supporting documentation, and coordinate the submission process.
Situation 3
Expat who owns foreign mutual funds triggering PFIC rules
Passive Foreign Investment Companies are subject to punitive default tax treatment under US law. Many expats discover they've been holding PFICs — foreign mutual funds or ETFs — without realizing the US tax consequences. We analyze your holdings and determine the least costly remediation path.
