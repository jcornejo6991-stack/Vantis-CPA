Common Situations

We've seen this before

Administrative friction is one of the most under-discussed parts of expat life. These are the scenarios we resolve regularly.

Situation 1
Expat whose US bank closed their account due to a foreign address
This happens more often than it should. Banks cite FATCA compliance concerns and close accounts without much warning. We help you get re-established with a US address and refer you to financial institutions that specifically serve expats without threatening account closure.
Situation 2
American abroad who needs IRS notices forwarded and answered
An IRS notice that goes to the wrong address — or sits unanswered — escalates into a much bigger problem. We receive, scan, and evaluate every notice, then advise on response timelines and handle replies where appropriate.
Situation 3
Expat needing notarized documents for a foreign government
Foreign governments frequently require notarized US documents — birth certificates, financial statements, tax returns. We coordinate the notarization and apostille process without requiring you to return to the US.
Related Issues

Connected compliance areas

Administrative support often surfaces compliance questions in these related areas. We handle them as part of a complete expat engagement.

Compliance Issue
FATCA Reporting Requirements
FATCA requires foreign financial institutions to report US account holders to the IRS — and requires US persons with significant foreign financial assets to file Form 8938. Understanding both sides prevents surprises.

Disclosure Issue
Foreign Bank Account Disclosure
If you have foreign financial accounts that in aggregate exceeded $10,000 at any point in the year, FBAR filing is required — regardless of whether any tax is owed. The penalties for non-filing are disproportionately severe.
