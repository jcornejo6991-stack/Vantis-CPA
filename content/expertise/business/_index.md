  - title: "Tax and Accounting for Owner-Operated Businesses"
    body: |
      Most small business owners pay more in self-employment tax than they need to, keep books that make tax season harder than it should be, and make financial decisions without knowing the tax consequence until the return is filed. The structure of the business, how the owner is paid, and how the books are kept all affect the tax outcome — and all of them can be improved. We work with owner-operated businesses to build the accounting and tax structure that reduces what you owe and keeps the business organized year-round.

  - title: "The Tax Advantage for Business Owners"
    body: |
      The tax code treats business owners differently than employees, and mostly in ways that favor the owner. Business expenses reduce taxable income directly. The entity structure determines how self-employment tax applies to the owner's earnings. Timing decisions around income and deductions can shift liability between years. Retirement contributions can shelter a significant portion of income from tax entirely.

      None of these benefits are automatic. They require knowing which ones apply, using them correctly, and making decisions throughout the year rather than only when the return is being prepared. A business that is well-organized and working with a CPA year-round pays less and has fewer surprises than one that treats accounting as a once-a-year event.
  - title: "Local Businesses"
    body: |
      Retail stores, trade contractors, and professional practices need a CPA who understands the business throughout the year, not just at tax time. That means bookkeeping that stays current, payroll that runs on schedule, entity structure that makes sense for the owner's situation, and tax filings that account for how the business is actually run.

      We work with stores, trades (electricians, plumbers, landscapers, builders), and professional practices (medical, legal, dental, consulting) that want a CPA who stays involved through the year and can coordinate the books, payroll, and tax side together.
  - title: "Online Businesses"
    body: |
      Selling online creates a specific kind of accounting complexity. Revenue arrives through multiple platforms with different settlement schedules, 1099-Ks that do not always match gross sales, and cost of goods that need to be tracked against imported inventory. Sales tax nexus expands automatically as volume grows across states, often without the seller realizing it.

      We work with Amazon and Shopify sellers, TikTok Shop merchants, Etsy and eBay resellers, dropshippers sourcing inventory from overseas suppliers, content creators monetizing through platforms, digital product sellers, and other businesses that operate primarily through online channels. The common thread is income that arrives from multiple sources and needs to be reconciled, reported, and planned around correctly.
