Common Situations

Sound familiar?

Sales tax and 1099 penalties are among the most common — and most avoidable — compliance issues we see in South Florida businesses.

E-Commerce / Online Store

Selling in multiple states and potentially past nexus thresholds

Since the South Dakota v. Wayfair ruling, you can owe sales tax in states where you've never had a physical presence. If you're selling $100k+ or 200+ transactions in any state, you likely have economic nexus there — and you need to be registered and filing, or you're accumulating back taxes and penalties.

Contractor / Trades

Paid 10+ subs last year — no 1099s filed, no W-9s collected

You used subcontractors throughout the year but didn't collect W-9s and haven't filed any 1099-NECs. The January 31 deadline passed. Now you're exposed to penalties per missing form — and if the IRS cross-references contractor income against your deductions, you'll have questions to answer.

Restaurant

Catering revenue with no clarity on Florida sales tax obligations

Florida's sales tax rules for food service are complicated: grocery items versus prepared food, on-premise versus off-premise, catering versus retail. Your catering revenue may be taxable in ways your regular dine-in sales are not. We sort out what's taxable and keep your DR-15 filings accurate.
