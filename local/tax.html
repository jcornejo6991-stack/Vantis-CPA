Common Situations

Sound familiar?

These are the situations we see most often with South Florida business owners — and fix.

Contractor

Paying full self-employment tax with no entity structure

You're netting $80k a year as a 1099 contractor and paying 15.3% SE tax on every dollar. An S-Corp election combined with reasonable compensation planning could cut that bill significantly. Someone just needs to run the math.

Restaurant Owner

No year-end planning — surprise $40k tax bill in April

Your restaurant had a strong year, but your old accountant didn't flag anything until they were preparing the return. Retirement plan contributions, equipment purchases, and timing strategies that could have helped are now off the table.

Real Estate Investor

Missing depreciation deductions on rental properties

You own rentals in Broward or Miami-Dade but have never had a cost segregation study done. Depreciation is being calculated the slow way — over 27.5 years — instead of front-loaded in year one through bonus depreciation.
