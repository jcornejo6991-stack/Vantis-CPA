Common Situations

Sound familiar?

These are the entity structure problems we solve most often for South Florida business owners.

Sole Proprietor

Netting $60k+ with no entity — paying full SE tax

You've been running your business as a sole proprietor or single-member LLC with no S-Corp election. At $70k net profit, an S-Corp election with reasonable compensation can save $5,000–$8,000 per year in self-employment tax. The window to elect for the current year closes faster than most people realize.

LLC Owner

Missed the S-Corp election deadline — stuck for another year

The default deadline to file Form 2553 for the current tax year is March 15. If you missed it, you can sometimes file late — but only with IRS reasonable cause relief. We've navigated this before and know when late election is viable.

Contractor / Multi-Venture

Multiple income streams that need a holding company structure

You have a contracting business, a rental property, and a side consulting practice — all lumped together or filed separately with no coordination. A proper holding company structure separates liability, simplifies taxes, and makes your finances easier to manage and sell.
