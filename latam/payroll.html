Common Situations

Businesses we work with regularly

Foreign-owned US businesses encounter payroll scenarios that domestic CPAs rarely see. Here are the situations we navigate most often.

🇲🇽

Mexican company with a US subsidiary hiring its first American employee

A Mexico-based business opens a Florida subsidiary and hires a US citizen as a regional manager. We register the entity as an employer, configure federal and Florida payroll withholding, and set up W-2 processing — properly segregated from the Mexican parent's payroll obligations.

🍽️

Foreign-owned restaurant needing compliant payroll

A LATAM family owns a restaurant in Florida with a mix of US citizen and NRA employees. Some workers have SSNs; others are working on visas and need ITIN-based payroll setup. We configure each worker correctly and ensure the right withholding rates apply to each paycheck.

💼

LATAM entrepreneur paying themselves from a US LLC

A nonresident alien who owns a US LLC wants to receive compensation from the entity. We analyze the correct characterization — guaranteed payment, W-2 wage, or owner's draw — and set up the appropriate withholding structure to avoid IRS underpayment issues.
