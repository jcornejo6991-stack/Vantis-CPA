Common Situations

Clients we work with regularly

Foreign-owned US entities come with specific bookkeeping challenges. Here are the scenarios we navigate most often.

🇨🇴

Colombian family with a US rental property

A family based in Bogotá owns a Florida condo producing rental income. Their US LLC needs monthly expense tracking — mortgage interest, HOA fees, repairs — organized into a 1040-NR ready ledger each year with proper FIRPTA documentation on hand.

🌎

LATAM holding company with multiple US subsidiaries

A Latin American family office with two US LLCs — one holding real estate, one operating a business — needs consolidated bookkeeping that tracks intercompany transfers between entities and between the US subsidiaries and the foreign parent.

⚠️

Foreign-owned LLC with undocumented intercompany loans

A LATAM entrepreneur transferred funds from abroad into a US LLC without formal loan documentation. These undocumented transfers are reportable transactions under Form 5472 — we reconstruct the records and establish proper documentation going forward.
