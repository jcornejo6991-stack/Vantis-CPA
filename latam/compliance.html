What's Included

Cross-Border Compliance & Disclosure Services

A complete compliance package for Latin American families with US exposure — covering every federal disclosure obligation from foreign accounts to real estate withholding.

🏦

FBAR — FinCEN Form 114

Annual filing of the Report of Foreign Bank and Financial Accounts for US persons with aggregate foreign account balances exceeding $10,000 — including accounts in Mexico, Colombia, Venezuela, Brazil, and throughout Latin America.

📋

FATCA — Form 8938

Statement of Specified Foreign Financial Assets filed with your annual tax return — required for US taxpayers with foreign financial assets above the applicable threshold, with higher penalties than FBAR for non-compliance.

🏢

Form 5472 — Foreign-Owned US Entities

Annual information return required for US LLCs and corporations with a 25% or greater foreign owner — filing of which carries a minimum $25,000 penalty per missed or incorrect return.

🏠

FIRPTA Withholding Certificates (Form 8288-B)

Application to the IRS to reduce or eliminate the mandatory 15% withholding on US real estate sales by foreign nationals — submitted before or at closing to avoid over-withholding on the proceeds.

🔄

ITIN Applications & Renewals

Individual Taxpayer Identification Number applications for foreign nationals who need a US tax ID — including renewals for expired ITINs that can block refunds and trigger IRS compliance flags.

🌐

Foreign Trust Reporting — Form 3520

Annual reporting for US persons with ownership or beneficial interests in foreign trusts — including transfers to foreign trusts and distributions received, which carry penalties of up to 35% of unreported amounts.

Common Situations

Families we've helped get current

Cross-border compliance failures are common and often unintentional. Here are the situations we see most frequently among Latin American families with US ties.

🇻🇪

Venezuelan family with accounts in the US and abroad exceeding FBAR thresholds

A Venezuelan family holds accounts at a US bank and in a Panamanian private bank — combined balance exceeds $10,000 at points during the year. Neither account was reported on an FBAR. We assess the years of exposure, file the delinquent FBARs under the IRS's streamlined procedures, and establish an annual filing routine going forward.

🇨🇴

Colombian investor selling US property subject to FIRPTA withholding

A Colombian national is under contract to sell a Florida property. Without a Form 8288-B withholding certificate, the buyer's closing agent will withhold 15% of the gross sales price at closing — regardless of actual gain. We file the application to reduce withholding to the actual tax liability before closing, protecting the client's proceeds.

🌎

LATAM client with a foreign trust requiring Form 3520

A Latin American family has beneficial interests in a family trust established in Panama. As US persons with interests in a foreign trust, they are required to file Form 3520 annually — a disclosure most CPAs overlook entirely. We identify the filing obligation, reconstruct any missed years, and file the required returns with the IRS.
