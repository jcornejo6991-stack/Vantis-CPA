Common situations we handle

The surprises that catch families off guard.

Most clients come to us after an advisor, a closing attorney, or a referral tells them something has gone wrong. We would rather meet you before that happens.

Withholding risk: up to 15% of gross sale

FIRPTA — Selling US Real Estate

When a foreign person sells US real estate, the buyer must withhold 15% of the gross sale price under FIRPTA — not just the gain. A $500,000 sale means $75,000 withheld. With proper planning and filing, this can often be reduced or recovered. Most sellers don't know until closing.

Learn more
Penalty: $25,000 per missed filing

Form 5472 — Foreign-Owned US Entities

If you own 25% or more of a US corporation or LLC, Form 5472 must be filed annually. The penalty for missing it is $25,000. This applies to US real estate held in LLCs, US operating companies, and investment vehicles.
