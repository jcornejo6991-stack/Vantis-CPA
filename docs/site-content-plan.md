# Site Content Plan

## Goal

Build the site as a coordinated content system instead of a set of isolated pages.

The site should answer three different kinds of questions:

1. `What do you do?`
Handled by service pages.
2. `Do you work with people like me?`
Handled by expertise pages.
3. `Can you answer this specific tax question?`
Handled by resource pages.

That split should drive all future copy, internal links, and SEO targeting.

## Core Page Roles

### Services

Service pages should target broad commercial-intent searches.

Primary job:
- explain the service
- describe what is included
- show who it is for
- create a path to contact

Current service pages:
- `Tax Strategy`
- `Tax Compliance`
- `International Tax`
- `Bookkeeping`
- `Payroll`
- `Sales Tax`
- `Back-Office Services`

Working rule:
Service pages answer `what we do`.

### Expertise

Expertise pages should target audience-specific or situation-specific intent.

Primary job:
- speak to a specific type of client
- show the problems they face
- connect that audience to the right services
- reinforce vertical authority

Current expertise pages:
- `Founders & Startups`
- `U.S. Expats`
- `Foreign Business and Investors`
- `Local Businesses`
- `Outbound U.S. Businesses`
- `Real Estate Owners`

Working rule:
Expertise pages answer `who we do it for`.

### Resources

Resource pages should target narrower informational searches and support topical authority.

Primary job:
- answer specific tax questions
- capture long-tail organic traffic
- support service and expertise pages with internal links

Current resource clusters:
- `business-tax`
- `individual-tax`
- `general-tax-compliance`
- `international-tax`
- `latam-inbound`
- `real-estate-tax`
- `ecommerce-sellers`

Working rule:
Resource pages answer `specific search questions`.

## Overlap Guardrails

The pages should reinforce each other, not compete with each other.

### Services vs Expertise

Use this split:

- Service pages describe the work itself.
- Expertise pages describe the client type and the version of that work that applies to them.

Example:
- `International Tax` explains cross-border tax work broadly.
- `U.S. Expats` explains that work for Americans abroad.
- `Foreign Business and Investors` explains that work for inbound foreign owners.
- `Outbound U.S. Businesses` explains that work for U.S. businesses operating abroad.

### Services vs Resources

Use this split:

- Service pages should not try to answer every detailed tax question.
- Resource pages should answer the specific questions in depth and link back to the service that solves them.

Example:
- `Tax Compliance` mentions `Schedule D`, `Schedule E`, quarterly estimates, and extensions.
- resource articles answer the narrower questions behind those topics.

### Expertise vs Resources

Use this split:

- Expertise pages describe the client and common pain points.
- Resource pages answer the individual tactical questions that client is likely to search.

Example:
- `Real Estate Owners` links to:
  - `1031 Exchange Guide`
  - `Real Estate Professional Status`
  - `Short-Term Rental Taxes`

## Standard Page Structures

### Service Page Structure

Recommended order:

1. Intro / framing
2. What the service includes
3. Who this is for
4. Ongoing support / process / how it works
5. High-value services list
6. Who We Serve
7. Related Reading

Writing rule:
Keep the page commercially focused.

### Expertise Page Structure

Recommended order:

1. Audience-specific framing
2. What this audience usually needs
3. Who this is for
4. Ongoing support / planning angle
5. High-value services list
6. Our Services
7. Related Reading

Writing rule:
Keep the audience front and center.

### Resource Page Structure

Recommended order:

1. Clear search-intent title
2. Fast answer / framing
3. Detailed explanation
4. Common mistakes / planning angle
5. FAQ
6. CTA back to service or contact

Writing rule:
Solve the search query clearly, then move the reader toward the relevant service.

## Topic Coverage Principles

Future content should stay inside the site’s strongest themes:

- tax strategy
- tax compliance
- international tax
- bookkeeping
- payroll
- sales tax
- entity setup / back-office support
- founders
- expats
- inbound foreign owners
- outbound U.S. businesses
- local businesses
- real estate owners

Avoid building topical branches that are too niche or disconnected from the rest of the site unless they clearly support a core vertical.

Examples to avoid overemphasizing:
- transfer pricing
- R&D credits
- highly specialized corporate tax topics that do not match the current service mix

## Working Rule for Future Edits

Before editing a page, answer:

1. Is this page supposed to explain `what we do`, `who we do it for`, or `a specific tax question`?
2. What is the primary search intent?
3. Which related pages should this one support?
4. Is the page drifting into another page’s job?

If those four answers are clear, the page should stay aligned with the rest of the site.
